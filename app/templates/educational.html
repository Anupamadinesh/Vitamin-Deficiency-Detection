🌞 Daily Health Tip

Stay hydrated and get sunlight for Vitamin D!

📘 Learning Corner - Fun Facts
  • Vitamin D is essential for absorbing calcium and building strong bones.
  • Vitamin A helps prevent night blindness and boosts immunity.
  • Vitamin C supports wound healing and collagen production.
  • Vitamin K helps in blood clotting and bone metabolism.Found in green leafy vegetables like spinach and kale. Note: This project does not detect Vitamin K deficiency, as it usually cannot be identified through facial features.
  • Vitamin B12 boosts brain function and energy levels.
  • Vitamin E acts as an antioxidant, helping protect cells from damage.
📘 Detailed Vitamin Explanations

Role: Vitamin A is important for vision, immune function, and skin health.

Deficiency Symptoms: Night blindness, dry skin, increased susceptibility to infections.

Food Sources: Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, liver, and egg yolks.

Role: Vitamin B complex helps in energy production and brain function.

Deficiency Symptoms: Fatigue, memory loss, irritability, and numbness in hands and feet.

Food Sources: Whole grains, meat, eggs, and legumes.

Role: Helps convert food into energy and supports nerve function.

Deficiency Symptoms: Fatigue, irritability, and nerve damage (beriberi).

Food Sources: Whole grains, pork, and beans.

Role: Important for cell division and the formation of red blood cells.

Deficiency Symptoms: Fatigue, weakness, and poor growth in infants.

Food Sources: Leafy greens, citrus fruits, beans, and fortified cereals.

Role: Vitamin B12 supports the formation of red blood cells, and nerve function, and helps in DNA synthesis.

Deficiency Symptoms: Fatigue, weakness, anemia, nerve damage, and cognitive decline.

Food Sources: Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy products.

Role: Vitamin D helps with calcium absorption and bone health.

Deficiency Symptoms: Rickets in children, osteoporosis in adults, and muscle weakness.

Food Sources: Sunlight, fatty fish, fortified milk, and eggs.

Role: Vitamin C is essential for collagen production, immune function, and the healing of wounds.

Deficiency Symptoms: Scurvy, weakened immune response, and delayed wound healing.

Food Sources: Citrus fruits, strawberries, bell peppers, and broccoli.

Role: Vitamin E is an antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage and supports immune function.

Deficiency Symptoms: Muscle weakness, vision problems, and impaired immune function.

Food Sources: Nuts, seeds, spinach, and vegetable oils.
